ShowMe Travel

An individual mobile UI Case Study

Case Study

Overview:

The objective of this project is to design a mobile travel application that fills a user need. Through user research, I discovered an unmet need in the business travel world - what to do in the down time between meetings and appointments.

My Role:

UX/UI Designer

Duration:

4 Weeks

Deliverables:

User Interviews, User Personas, Storyboards, Competitive Analysis, User Flows, Wireframes, Lo-fidelity and iOS Mock-ups

Problem:

Business travelers need to find ways to relax during their downtime while on the road. Traditional travel apps don't adequately address the unique needs of business travelers looking for quick activities.

Solution:

The ShowMe Travel mobile travel application is designed to meet the pain points of business travelers by providing access to quick, local events and activities that can be explored during downtime between meetings and appointments.

Goal Fish

A group mobile UI Case Study

Case Study

Overview:

This group project focused on creating a mobile application that solved a user need. Along with my partner, we focused on creating an application that helps people create, track and accomplish personal goals.

Our Roles:

Haley Ugwuibe & Michele Heckmann UX/UI Designers

Duration:

3 Weeks

Deliverables:

User Interviews, User Personas, Storyboards, Competitive Analysis, User Flows, Wireframes, Lo-fidelity and iOS Mock-ups, Clickable Prototypes

Problem:

Studies show that 92% of people that set personal goals fail to reach their objectives. There are many reasons why people often fail but one common issue is that without commiting the goals to paper, it is easy to lose sight of the goals and the progress that has been made.

Solution:

"Give a man a fish and he eats for a day, teach a man to fish and he eats for a lifetime." What if we could teach a person to create goals that are achievable and give them the tools to succeed? That was our objective on this project.

US Department of Labor

An individual Government Website Redesign

Case Study

Overview:

Improve the user experience for an existing Government Website was the objective for this group project.

Our Roles:

Nada Alrefaei & Michele Heckmann UI Designers

Duration:

4 Weeks

Deliverables:

Competitive Analysis, Card Sorting, Information Architecture, Style Tiles, Style Sheets, Wireframes, Lo-fidelity and High-fidelity Prototypes

Problem:

In general, Government websites tend to be data intensive and not aesthetically pleasing. The U.S. Department of Labor website is packed with useful information for both employers and employees but most people do not use this site for important labor information. The reason, the information tends to be very technical and difficult to find on the site.

Solution:

Using Information Architecture and tools like card sorting, we uncovered the challenges with the current site and redesigned the content in ways that was more meaningful to both employees and employers.

The Gift of Music

A group non-profit 501(c)(3) Website Redesign

Case Study

Overview:

This group project was focused on redesigning a local non-profit 501(c)(3) website to improve the overall user experience.

Our Roles:

Nada Alrefaei, Ji Hu & Michele Heckmann UI Designers

Duration:

4 Weeks

Deliverables:

User Interviews, User Personas, Competitive Analysis, User Flows, Card Sorting, Style Guides, Social Media Guidelines, Lo-fidelity and High-fidelity Prototypes

Problem:

Driving donations and volunteerism is the goal of any non-profit orgsnization. Without being able to speak to The Gift of Music stakeholders, we focused on the challenges that typically face non-profits - getting and keeping donors and volunteers.

Solution:

Our research showed that in general donors want to know how their money is being is being spent. Specific to our non-profit, people felt that the cause was worthwhile but that there were bigger challenges facing youth today. Our challenge was to show the impact of after-school music programs and how they effect the lives of at-risk youth - both in the short-term and long-term.
